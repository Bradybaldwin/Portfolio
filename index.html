Hello, I'm
Brady Baldwin
I have a passion for research that helps better understand human cognition. My goal is to apply that understanding to make our everyday interactions with the digital world more intutitve.
I was born and raised in Salem, Oregon and completed my undergrad at Oregon State University with a double major in Psychology and New Media Communications. While at OSU I fostered a unique interest in the intersection between human cognition and virtual reality. I had the privilige of collaborating on several projects exploring this space, including work as a 3D modeler and Research Assistant for the Cognition and Action in Real and Virtual Environments (CARVE) Lab.
After graduating from OSU in Spring of 2020 I decided to pursue futher education in the HCI field by enrolling in the MHCI program at Carnegie Mellon University. During my first semester at CMU I took several HCI-focused courses tailored to increase my skills in research, design and programming. I developed familiarity with rapid prototyping and user testing, made use of many user research techniques including contextual inquiry and storyboarding, and got practice with integrating frameworks like Bootstrap and Greensock for front-end web development. I am currently in the second semester of the program and am continuing in my development with many of these skills. In addition I am working as a research assistant in the CMU OH! Lab, where I am developing and user testing VR environments built in Unity for a study on collaborative VR spaces. I am also currently a member of an MCHI Capstone team, a two-term collaborative project with Pandemic Professors focused on improving the pairing process between tutors and students.
